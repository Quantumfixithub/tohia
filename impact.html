Our Impact

At TOHAI, we measure success by lives reached, systems strengthened, and futures changed.

Our Approach

How We Measure Success

We measure success not by activities, but by lives protected.

Impact is not about the number of events we run or the materials we distribute. It's about the young people who now understand their genotype, the newborns who receive early diagnosis, the families connected to care, and the policies that will protect future generations.

Multiplier Effect

How Our Impact Grows

Prevention Creates Lasting Change

By intervening at the genotype awareness and newborn screening stages, TOHAI addresses the root causes of sickle cell disease burden rather than its downstream consequences.

  • Reduced child mortality rates
  • Lower long-term healthcare costs for families and government
  • Strengthened primary healthcare systems nationwide
  • National health system resilience and sustainability
  • Generational impact on family planning and health outcomes

This approach delivers not just individual benefits, but creates a multiplier effect across public health, maternal and child survival, and long-term economic productivity.
