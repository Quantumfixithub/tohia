Building a Nigeria Where Every Child Has a Healthier Start

Genotype education. Early screening. Stronger futures.

Tochi Health Access Initiative (TOHAI) is a non-profit public health organisation working to improve access to genotype education, early screening, referral pathways, and policy advocacy for sickle cell disease across Nigeria.

We exist to prevent unnecessary suffering and ensure that every young person has the knowledge and access they need to make informed health and life decisions.

About TOHAI

The Challenge We Address

Nigeria carries nearly 50% of the global burden of sickle cell disease, yet millions of young people remain unaware of their genotype, and most newborns do not receive early screening. This leads to preventable illness, complications, emotional trauma, and early death.

TOHAI works at the intersection of community engagement, healthcare partnerships, and public policy to change this reality. Our programmes are designed to be practical, scalable, and deeply rooted in community needs.

We believe access to life-saving health information and services should never depend on income, geography, or luck.

What We Do

Our Core Programmes

Youth Genotype Education

Nationwide education campaigns that empower young people with accurate knowledge about genotype, reproductive health, and prevention.

Community Screening Partnerships

Affordable and accessible screening delivered through mobile clinics, partner laboratories, and community health centres.

Early Care Referral Pathways

Linking diagnosed individuals and newborns to early treatment, counselling, and long-term care.

Policy Advocacy

Working with government institutions to establish universal newborn screening and sustainable public health financing.
