What We Do

Our Core Programmes

Programme 01

üéì Youth Genotype Education Programme

We deliver age-appropriate, culturally sensitive education programmes across schools, universities, faith institutions, and community centres nationwide.

Our sessions empower young people with knowledge about:

  • Genotype compatibility and what it means for future families
  • Informed relationship and reproductive health decisions
  • Family planning and early prevention strategies
  • The science of sickle cell disease in accessible language

This helps reduce new cases of sickle cell disease and promotes healthier life choices across generations.

Programme 02

üî¨ Community Screening Partnerships

We collaborate with hospitals, laboratories, and mobile clinics to deliver affordable genotype screening in underserved communities.

Our model:

  • Reduces cost barriers through partnerships and subsidies
  • Expands geographic access to remote and underserved areas
  • Builds trust within communities through local engagement
  • Encourages early testing for newborns and young people

Accessible screening is the foundation of prevention ‚Äî knowing your genotype changes everything.

Programme 03

üè• Referral Pathways & Early Care Access

We establish structured referral networks linking screening services to hospitals, treatment centres, and support services.

This ensures:

  • Timely diagnosis and immediate follow-up care
  • Early medical intervention for newborns and children
  • Improved long-term health outcomes
  • Ongoing care continuity and family support

Early diagnosis can reduce childhood mortality by up to 90% ‚Äî every connection we make saves lives.

Programme 04

üìã Policy Advocacy & Systems Change

We engage policymakers, government agencies, healthcare institutions, and civil society to advocate for universal newborn screening and stronger national sickle cell policies.

Our advocacy focuses on:

  • Policy reform for mandatory newborn screening
  • Public funding for screening programmes nationwide
  • Health system integration at primary healthcare level
  • National screening frameworks and implementation

Sustainable change requires policy ‚Äî we're building the systems that will protect future generations.
