Our Programs

Comprehensive initiatives designed to educate, screen, support, and advocate for sickle cell disease prevention and care across Nigeria.

Genotype Education & Awareness

Our flagship education program reaches young people, families, and communities with critical information about genotype compatibility and the importance of making informed reproductive health decisions.

Through interactive workshops, school outreach programs, youth-focused campaigns, and digital awareness initiatives, we ensure that every participant understands their genotype and its implications for their health and future family planning.

Early Screening Initiative

Early detection saves lives. Our screening initiative promotes accessible, affordable genotype and sickle cell testing for newborns, children, and young adults across Nigeria.

We partner with trusted healthcare providers, community health centers, and schools to organize screening drives that bring testing directly to underserved communities. Every screening comes with pre- and post-test counseling to ensure participants understand their results.

Referral & Support Pathways

A positive diagnosis is just the beginning. Our referral and support program ensures that individuals and families affected by sickle cell disease receive comprehensive, ongoing care and support.

We've built strong partnerships with healthcare facilities, genetic counselors, sickle cell foundations, and support groups to create seamless referral pathways. From initial diagnosis to long-term management, we walk alongside families every step of the way.

Policy Advocacy

Sustainable change requires strong policies. We engage policymakers, government agencies, and health authorities to strengthen national frameworks for sickle cell disease prevention and care.

Our advocacy efforts focus on making newborn screening mandatory, integrating genotype education into school curricula, improving access to care, and ensuring adequate funding for sickle cell programs nationwide.
