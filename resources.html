Genetic Inheritance

Sickle cell disease is inherited when both parents carry the sickle cell gene (AS or SS genotype).

Early Detection

Prevention is Possible

3

Get genetic counseling to understand compatibility and make informed decisions.

Screen Newborns

Ensure all newborns are screened within the first few days of life for early intervention.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is genotype?

Genotype refers to the genetic makeup inherited from both parents. In the context of sickle cell disease, it indicates whether you carry normal hemoglobin genes (AA), sickle cell trait (AS), or sickle cell disease (SS).

Can AS genotype marry AS genotype?

While legally possible, it is medically inadvisable. When two AS partners have children, there is a 25% chance each child will have SS (sickle cell disease), 50% chance of AS (carrier), and 25% chance of AA (normal).

How is sickle cell disease managed?

Early diagnosis, regular medical care, pain management, prevention of infections, adequate hydration, and family support are essential for managing sickle cell disease.

Where can I get tested?

Genotype testing is available at most hospitals, diagnostic laboratories, and health centers across Nigeria. TOHAI also partners with facilities to provide affordable screening.

Is there a cure for sickle cell disease?

While bone marrow transplant can cure sickle cell disease in some cases, prevention through genotype awareness and informed decision-making remains the most effective approach.
