Sickle Cell Disease: Myths & Facts

MYTH

Sickle cell disease only affects people of African descent

FACT

While more common in people of African ancestry, sickle cell disease also occurs in people from Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, and South Asian backgrounds.

MYTH

If both partners have AS genotype, all children will have sickle cell disease

FACT

When both partners are AS, there's a 25% chance each child will have SS (sickle cell disease), 50% chance of AS (carrier), and 25% chance of AA (no trait).

MYTH

People with AS genotype are sick

FACT

People with AS genotype (sickle cell trait) are generally healthy and do not have symptoms of sickle cell disease. They are carriers of the trait.

MYTH

Sickle cell disease can be cured easily

FACT

While there's no widely available cure, sickle cell disease can be managed with proper medical care. Bone marrow transplant is currently the only potential cure but has risks and limitations.
