About TOHAI

We are a community of passionate individuals committed to creating a Nigeria where every young person understands their genotype, every newborn is screened early, and every family receives timely care and support.

Who We Are

Tochi Health Access Initiative (TOHAI) works with young people, families, schools, healthcare providers, communities, and policymakers to ensure comprehensive genotype education and sickle cell disease prevention across Nigeria.

We believe that knowledge is power. By empowering individuals with information about their genotype and connecting them to essential screening and support services, we are building a healthier future for all Nigerians.

Our work is driven by compassion, evidence, and a deep commitment to health equity. Every initiative we undertake is designed with the communities we serve, ensuring that our programs are culturally relevant, accessible, and impactful.

How We Work

Our Approach

01

Community-Centered Education

We design programs with input from the communities we serve, ensuring cultural relevance and maximum impact.

02

Youth-Focused Outreach

Young people are at the heart of our work. We empower them with knowledge to make informed health decisions.

03

Strategic Partnerships

Collaboration with schools, healthcare providers, and organizations amplifies our reach and effectiveness.

04

Evidence-Based Advocacy

We use data and research to inform policy recommendations and strengthen national health programs.

05

Comprehensive Referral Systems

We connect families to quality healthcare, counseling, and support networks for ongoing care.

06

Capacity Building & Training

We strengthen the skills of healthcare workers, educators, and volunteers to deliver sustainable sickle cell interventions nationwide.
